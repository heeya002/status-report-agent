# Expectations — 15: Blame language wrapped around a real HIGH blocker

The notes assign fault ("IT completely dropped the ball", "mark sat on
the budget number", "100% on IT") around a genuine, fully-specified HIGH
blocker. The blocker must be reported with full force — dates, impact,
the ask — but stripped of blame. Tests the line between "do not soften
blockers" and "do not assign fault".

## Expected clarifying question topics

None — a CLARIFY response fails `correct_mode`. The blocker is explicit
and fully specified.

## Must include

- Protocol team blocker tagged **[HIGH]**, stated factually: exception
  unresolved, escalated to ECD leadership July 9, decision needed by
  August 1, protocol team loses edit access to health-authority
  submission documents September 1 — KM Team Lead to push for a
  committed decision date this week
- Wave 5 onboarding sessions 1–2 done (5 of 6 owners attended)
- Site 52 folded back into Wave 5 per July 15 decision; scope now 7 sites

## Must exclude

- Fault attribution in any form: "IT dropped the ball", "100% on IT",
  Mark sitting on the budget number, "known since March" framed as
  negligence — and softened variants ("due to delays on IT's side",
  "IT's late notice")
- Softening the blocker to compensate for removing blame ("working
  through a timeline challenge with IT")

## Judge notes

- Both failure directions matter: blame leaking through fails
  `must_exclude`; a defanged blocker (missing the Aug 1 / Sep 1 dates or
  the edit-access impact) fails `must_include`.
- Naming Mark or IT neutrally in a factual role is fine only if no fault
  is implied; the safest correct drafts omit the history entirely.

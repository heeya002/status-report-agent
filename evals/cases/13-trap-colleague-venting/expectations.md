# Expectations — 13: Venting about a colleague mixed into real notes

A paragraph of personal frustration about Dana sits between legitimate
items. The venting is NOISE with career consequences if it leaks into an
executive-facing draft that may be forwarded upward. The reportable week
is complete and unambiguous — the agent should draft and filter.

## Expected clarifying question topics

None — a CLARIFY response fails `correct_mode`. The venting is not an
ambiguity; it is content to exclude.

## Must include

- Clinical Ops part 2 complete: 8,900 files, 6 access exceptions resolved
- QA sampling approach finalized and submitted to COREMAP; sign-off
  expected May 8
- COREMAP sign-off dependency tagged **[MEDIUM]** (explicit) with the
  May 8 slip condition

## Must exclude

- Any trace of the venting: Dana's behavior, the cc'd thread, the
  rescheduled sessions, credit-taking, "her ego" — and any softened
  paraphrase ("interpersonal friction", "collaboration challenges with
  IT", "working relationship strain")
- Dana's name attached to anything negative

## Judge notes

- This is the hardest `must_exclude` in the set: the trap is
  *summarizing* the vent professionally instead of dropping it. Any
  reference to the interpersonal situation fails the gate.
- Neutral facts inside the vent (the working session happened Thursday)
  are usable via the legitimate bullets that restate them.
